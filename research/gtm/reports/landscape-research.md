## The Market in One Paragraph

The AI pitch deck analysis space is crowded but shallow. 20+ tools exist, but ~85-90% are founder-facing (help founders build better decks). The investor-facing niche — tools that help VCs screen and evaluate inbound deal flow faster — has only 5-7 players, none with dominant market share. No competitor uses a structured investor-derived checklist with stage-gating and a Verified/Quick Win/Gap scoring model. The closest competitors (VentureLens, DeckMatch, Pitch Deck Scanner) focus on data extraction and freeform analysis, not structured evaluation. This validates Venture Scale's positioning as the only tool built FROM the investor side with a real framework.

## Distribution Playbook

### The Granola Pattern (Proven for VC Tools)

Granola chose VCs as their beachhead because they "conduct a lot of calls, share a lot of notes, love to try new AI products, have a high willingness to pay, and have very influential networks." Reached $1.5B valuation through VC word of mouth alone.

**The consistent playbook across Granola, Harmonic, Superhuman:**
1. Solve a real, frequent pain point
2. Make the output shareable (every shared analysis = product demo)
3. Target VCs because of network density and influence
4. Let usage drive distribution, not marketing
5. Don't ask for anything — the relationship emerges from genuine value

### Alpha Distribution: 3 Phases

**Phase 1 (Week 1-2): Inner Circle — 10-15 VCs**
- Personal contacts. DM/text, not email.
- Framing: "I've been building something for deck screening — uses AI for structured analysis in under a minute. Try it on real decks, tell me what's missing."
- 30-second Loom showing a real (anonymized) analysis.

**Phase 2 (Week 3-4): Extended Network — 15-25 more**
- Warm intros from Phase 1. Ask each user: "Know anyone else who screens a lot of decks?"
- Watch signals: repeat usage > verbal praise.

**Phase 3 (Week 5-6): Community — 20-50 more**
- Post in 1-2 VC Slack communities (EVCA, Gen Z VCs).
- "I built this" post on X.

### Key User: The Associate/Analyst

Associates are the real buyer. They read 50-200 decks/month, spend 5-10 hours/week screening, and flag 5-15 for partner review. A tool that helps them screen in 30 seconds instead of 5 minutes is directly addressing their biggest pain point. Adoption goes bottom-up: associate → partner → firm-wide.

## What This Means for the Web App

The minimum viable web app needs:
1. PDF upload → stage select → structured analysis in <60 seconds
2. Output good enough that a VC forwards it to their partner
3. Clean, simple, no account required for alpha (or magic link at most)
4. Mobile-friendly output (VCs read on phones)
5. Privacy: decks should be deleteable/ephemeral

Can wait:
- Batch upload (first paid feature)
- CRM integration (Affinity eventually, but not alpha)
- Dashboard/history (nice-to-have)
- Custom checklists (premium feature)
